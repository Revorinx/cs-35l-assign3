1. Reworked the game rules so the board supports two phases: a placement phase where each player can place up to three pieces, and a movement phase that begins after all pieces have been placed
2. Added helper functions to calculate a winner, convert board indexes into row and column coordinates, and check whether two squares are adjacent, so the movement rules could be enforced in one place
3. Added movement validation so a player can only move one of their own pieces to an adjacent empty square, including diagonal movement
4. Added the center-square restriction (if a player has a piece in the center square, that turn must either win immediately or move that center piece out of the center)
5. Changed the board click behavior so the player can click one of their own pieces to select it during the movement phase and then click a legal destination square to complete the move
6. Added a visual highlight for the selected piece and updated the status text so the game tells the player whether they should place, select, or move a piece
